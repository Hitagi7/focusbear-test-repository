### Cyber Security Guidelines Questions
**What security measures do you currently follow, and where can you improve?**
- The security measures that I currently follow are using two-factor authentication (2FA) on all my my important accounts, updating my operating system and applications regularly, using strong, unique passwords for important applications, and using a virtual private network (VPN) when using public networks. I can even further improve my security measures by using a password manager to generate and store complex, non-repeating strings for every service I use.

**How can you make secure behaviour a habit rather than an afterthought?**
- I can make a secure behaviour a habit rather than an afterthought by integrating security checks to every commit I make, ensuring that no sensitive API keys or credentials are ever hard-coded into my codebases. Additionally, I will adopt the habit of physically locking my workstation every time I step away—even in a home environment—to ensure that "security-first" becomes a muscle memory reaction rather than a conscious decision.

**What steps will you take to ensure your passwords and accounts are secure?**
- The steps that I will take to ensure my passwords and accounts are secure are: Migrate all my credentials to a dedicated password manager to generate and store complex, non-repeating strings for every service I use. Prioritize the use of hardware-based or app-based 2FA tokens over SMS-based codes to protect against SIM-swapping attacks. Perform a monthly review of "Logged In Devices" on my critical accounts to spot any unauthorized access.

**What would you do if you suspected a security breach or suspicious activity on your account?**
- If I suspect a security breach or suspicious activity on my account, is that I would immediately follow the protocol of the company by reporting it to my supervisor and the IT security lead while simultaneously changing my master password from a known clean device. Furthermore, I would document the specific activity I observed, such as unrecognized login locations or unauthorized Git commits, to provide a clear trail for the security team to investigate and mitigate any potential data exposure.
